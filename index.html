üí∞ Why Track Accounts Receivable?

Revenue isn't real until it's in your bank account. An unpaid invoice is just a promise. The longer it goes unpaid, the less likely you'll ever see that money.

AR aging shows you exactly where your money is stuck and who needs a nudge (or a harder conversation).

üìä The scary stat: Invoices unpaid after 90 days have only a 73% chance of collection. After 120 days, it drops to 57%. Time is money ‚Äî literally.

üíº Pro Tips From the Field

  • Invoice immediately: Don't wait until month-end. Send invoices the day work is delivered. Delays in invoicing = delays in payment.
  • Set clear terms upfront: "Net 30" should be in your contract AND on your invoice. No ambiguity about when payment is due.
  • Offer easy payment: Accept credit cards, bank transfer, PayPal. The easier you make it, the faster they pay.
  • Require deposits: For new clients or large projects, require 50% upfront. You'll never chase that half.
  • Stop work at 60 days: Don't keep delivering while they owe you money. It rarely improves the situation.
  • Track your DSO: Days Sales Outstanding (average collection time) should be under 45 days. If it's climbing, you have a process problem.
  • Fire chronic late-payers: A client who always pays 90+ days late is costing you money and stress. Replace them.
